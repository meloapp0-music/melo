# Melo — Reels Production Guide

End-to-end: what reels to make, how to record them, what tools, how to
edit, how to post. Built for one person, ~45 min/week.

Companion to `marketing/social-schedule.md` (cadence + hashtags) and
`marketing/social-calendar-2026-05.md` (the calendar — Monday is the
Reel slot).

**The strategy:** mostly app/screen-recording reels (no face, low
effort), with the occasional founder-on-camera piece. Concepts 1–6
need no face at all. 7–9 are founder reels for when you have the
energy.

---

## Part 1 — The toolkit (set up once)

| Tool | Cost | What it's for |
|---|---|---|
| **iPhone Screen Recording** | free, built-in | Capturing the app — your primary footage source |
| **OpusClip** | ~$9.50/mo | Auto-cuts a long recording into captioned, hook-ready clips |
| **CapCut** | free | Manual edits, founder reels, trending audio, text overlays |
| **TikTok + Instagram** | free | Post the SAME cut natively to both |

One-time: add Screen Recording to your iPhone Control Center
(Settings → Control Center → add "Screen Recording"). Sign up for
OpusClip and CapCut.

## Part 2 — Recording

### App / screen reels (concepts 1–6)
1. Open Melo, get to the screen you want **before** you start recording
2. Control Center → tap the **screen-record** button → wait for the
   3-2-1
3. **Move slowly and deliberately** — swipe through Wrapped, scroll the
   Home screen, type into LogShow. Slow reads well on video; fast looks
   glitchy.
4. Record **30–90 seconds** of raw footage. More than you need — you'll
   trim.
5. Stop the recording (tap the red bar). It saves to Photos.

**Before recording:** full battery, no notifications (Do Not Disturb
on), clean status bar. A junky status bar makes a reel look amateur.

### Founder reels (concepts 7–9)
- Phone propped on anything stable, **vertical**, natural light (face a
  window)
- Film in **15–30 second chunks** — easier to nail, easier to edit
- POV b-roll counts as "founder content": your laptop while coding,
  the walk to a venue, a ticket in hand, the crowd. You don't have to
  talk to camera the whole time.

## Part 3 — Editing

### The OpusClip path (fastest — use this by default)
1. Go to opusclip.com → upload your raw screen recording
2. It returns **5–8 clips** with captions and hooks auto-added
3. Scan them, **keep the best 1–3**, discard the generic ones
4. Download. Done.

### The CapCut path (when you want manual control — founder reels)
1. New project → import your clips
2. Trim to the good parts
3. Add a **text hook on the very first frame** (see each concept's hook
   line below)
4. Add **trending audio** (CapCut shows what's trending; or pull a
   sound from TikTok directly)
5. Export at **1080×1920**

### The one rule that matters most
**The first 1 second is the whole reel.** Lead with the hook frame —
the boldest visual + the hook text — immediately. No logo intro, no
slow build. If second one is boring, no one sees second two.

## Part 4 — The reel library (9 ready-to-shoot concepts)

Each has a hook (the text on frame 1), a shot list, a caption, and
hashtags. Use ≤7 hashtags.

### App / screen reels — no face needed

**1. A year of concerts on a map**
- Hook text: `I went to 30 shows last year. here's where 🗺️`
- Shots: screen-record Wrapped → swipe to the animated map slide → let
  the route fully draw, miles counter ticking → hold 2s on the finished
  map
- Caption: `Every venue, every mile, one map. Melo Wrapped. 🎶 Free on iOS — link in bio.`
- Hashtags: `#concertlife #livemusic #wrappedseason #concertdiary #musicapp`
- Audio: trending instrumental with a build

**2. What's your concert personality?**
- Hook text: `apparently i'm an Indie Night Owl 👀`
- Shots: screen-record the kinetic Personality slide — the "you're" →
  archetype → line reveal
- Caption: `What does your concert history say about you? 🎭 Comment your archetype ⬇️`
- Hashtags: `#concertvibes #livemusic #musicpersonality #concertdiary`
- Audio: punchy, curious trending sound

**3. Don't type your setlists**
- Hook text: `you should never type a setlist again`
- Shots: screen-record LogShow → type an artist → pick the real show
  from the dropdown → the setlist auto-fills song by song
- Caption: `Melo pulls the setlist for you. Every song, every show, zero typing. 🎤`
- Hashtags: `#livemusic #setlist #concertlife #musicapp #concertdiary`
- Audio: clean, satisfying

**4. Log a show in 15 seconds**
- Hook text: `logging a concert in real time ⏱️`
- Shots: fast-cut screen recording — open LogShow, type artist, pick
  the show, set score, tap vibes, hit Log → the ✓ confirmation
- Caption: `15 seconds. One show, logged forever. 🎶`
- Hashtags: `#concertlife #livemusic #musicapp #concertdiary #showseason`
- Audio: fast, energetic trending

**5. Rank every show you've been to**
- Hook text: `ranking every concert i've ever been to`
- Shots: screen-record starring favorites (★), the Rankings page, the
  scores
- Caption: `Your shows, ranked. What's your #1? ⬇️`
- Hashtags: `#concertlife #livemusic #concertdiary #musiclover`
- Audio: trending

**6. POV: you remember every concert**
- Hook text: `POV: you actually remember every show you've been to`
- Shots: slow scroll of the Home screen — stats, the Wrapped card, the
  show collection
- Caption: `Every show, every setlist, every memory. Kept. 🎶 Free on iOS.`
- Hashtags: `#concertlife #livemusic #concertmemories #concertdiary #musicapp`
- Audio: warm, nostalgic trending

### Founder reels — face / voice

**7. Why I built Melo**
- Hook (you, to camera): `I built an app because I kept forgetting which concerts I'd been to.`
- Beats (30–45s): the problem (the broken Notes doc, blanking on "was
  that show good?") → the moment you decided to build it → what Melo
  does now → `free on iOS, link in bio`
- B-roll: drop 2–3 short Melo screen recordings over your voice
- Caption: `the origin story. building Melo 🎶`
- Hashtags: `#buildinpublic #indiehacker #concertlife #livemusic #soloapp`

**8. Day in the life building a concert app solo**
- Hook text: `day in the life building a concert app by myself`
- Shots: POV b-roll — laptop/coding, coffee, the train or walk to a
  venue, the show, opening Melo to log it after
- Caption: `build the app → use the app → repeat 🎶`
- Hashtags: `#buildinpublic #indiehacker #dayinthelife #concertlife #soloapp`
- Audio: vlog-style trending

**9. Things I shipped this week**
- Hook text: `things I shipped in Melo this week 🚀`
- Shots: quick screen recordings of each new feature, with a text label
  naming each one
- Caption: `shipping every week. what should I build next? ⬇️`
- Hashtags: `#buildinpublic #indiehacker #appdev #concertlife #soloapp`
- Audio: upbeat trending

## Part 5 — Posting + the weekly routine

### Posting
- **One reel per week, minimum.** Monday is the Reel slot on the
  calendar.
- Cross-post the **exact same cut** to **TikTok and IG Reels** — no
  need to re-edit. Post natively in each app (don't share a TikTok with
  the watermark to IG — IG suppresses it).
- Post **5–7pm** in your timezone.
- **Reply to every comment in the first hour.** Comment velocity is the
  single biggest algorithm signal — block out that hour.

### The Sunday batch (~45 min)
1. Record source footage (5 min) — one or two screen recordings of the
   week's concept
2. OpusClip it (10 min) — upload, get clips back
3. Pick the best 1–3 (5 min)
4. Caption from the concept above (5 min)
5. Schedule for Monday (or post live Monday)

### The momentum rule
If a reel hits **10x your normal views** — drop the calendar and make
**3 more in that exact style** that week. Algorithms reward riding a
winner. Don't let it cool off.

### Don't
- No reel that's just a static image with music — that's a feed post
- No slow intros — hook on frame 1
- No more than 7 hashtags
- No posting the watermarked TikTok export to IG
